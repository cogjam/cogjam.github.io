Project Idea

My project, OWNED, will be a responsive web-app built built for the skateboarding community globally. The web-app will be map-based and let skaters compete for 'best trick at spot' by having footage of their trick voted on by users. The app will require a back-end database and front-end user interface. The interface will allow users to add links to videos for locations, and will let viewers browse a map to see tricks at different skating spots. When viewing a location, users can vote on tricks, and videos will be ranked according to community votes.

The web-app will be useful for the skateboarding community as it will allow them to discover new good spots for skating, and for seeing different features within spots that they may not have considered using before. The app will be interesting to users as it will allow them to watch videos of the locations they may know, and will facilitate local and global rivalry.

The app will be a web-based responsive app primarily centered around a map interface with embedded videos and voting buttons. It will be a mobile-first experience as many users (primarily teenagers) will be using the app on their phones, with peers, at skating locations.

The users will not need to register or log-in to vote, rather to restrict voting to one vote per spot, the app will reference the users IP to a database.

The app will be built on a database that stores information such as the coordinates of a spot, links to uploaded videos, and votes on videos. At this stage, for the app there will be no options for users to add comments to videos, except from directly on the linked YouTube video itself.

The web-app will be built using Webflow, a web-based WYSIWYG front-end development tool, and MapBox, a mapping api. Data will be stored using Airtable, a web-based database tool, and referenced using Integromat, a tool that provides a visual interface for working with APIs.

The purpose for using propitiatory tools as mentioned is that of building the app using a Minimum Viable Product MVP methodology. Building a MVP will allow me to develop a product quickly (days instead of weeks or months) and will let me see which features (if any) users appreciate before investing heavily into their production.

The web-app will require a good understanding of working with all the tools outlined above (Webflow, MapBox, Airtable and Integromat), so will need the development team to understand basic front-end web-development skills (HTML and CSS), basic mapping concepts (how coordinates are referenced and called, how MapBox renders mapping layers etc.) and how the API integration works with a tool such as Integromat.

If this product is successful it will allow skateboarders to have an additional connection with their local skateboarding environment, and it's community. The app will encourage skateboarders to reconsider their built environment, and see new spots in their cities/towns. The app will serve to bring together potentially isolated young people, as it will encourage users to return to a spot many times to master a trick to 'OWN' that location. A battle over a spot would likely have two or more people returning often to 'RECLAIM' the spot, and like common in skateboarding, would foster close social connection over friendly rivalry.
